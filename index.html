Main Challenges in Learning

Neither school nor university teaches us how to study material properly. We prepare for exams and memorize tickets. We train to solve monotonous problems to perform better on tests, but often this does not give us real knowledge. Cramming quickly evaporates and is of no use.

Conclusion: Learning is also a skill, but somehow it is rarely taught. What should we do about it?

Specific techniques and exercises help change the approach to learning, making it effective and exciting. The same techniques are applied, for example, in the learning process at Practicum.

Learning Techniques

Five practices from Barbara Oakley

  • Two Types of Attention

    Deep knowledge arises when focused and diffused thinking are alternated.

  • Recall

    Recall what you've learned — it helps connect disjointed memory chunks.

  • Interleaving

    Study multiple skills simultaneously; they enrich each other.

  • Questions

    While listening to a teacher, come up with a good question that really concerns you.

  • Illusion of Competence

    Repeat to yourself, write down, tell a friend: you just think you’ve mastered the new topic.

The Story of Barbara Oakley

From childhood, Barbara struggled with mathematics. She considered herself a complete humanitarian, not particularly intelligent. In the army, she studied Russian to get a bonus, and did it so successfully that she was promoted to commander. However, to advance further, she needed to pass mathematics. That’s when Barbara developed her own approach to the exact sciences. It turns out that if something is "hard to learn," your hard-earned knowledge is much deeper than that of those who understand everything at first glance.
